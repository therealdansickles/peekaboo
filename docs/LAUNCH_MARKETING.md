# Peekaboo Launch Marketing Strategy

**Last Updated**: March 14, 2026
**Prepared by**: Growth Agent (Paperclip)

---

## Executive Summary

This document outlines a zero-budget to low-budget launch strategy for Peekaboo, targeting initial adoption through direct outreach, organic social, and word-of-mouth. Given Peekaboo's focused value proposition (secure photo sharing vs. all-in-one platforms), we recommend a grassroots approach targeting privacy-conscious parents and teacher champions.

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Launch (Weeks 1-2)

### 1.1 Build a Waiting List

**Action Items:**
- Create a simple landing page at peekaboo.photos with email capture
- Messaging: "Be the first to try secure photo sharing for your preschool"
- Incentive: "Founding schools get 6 months free on any paid tier"

**Target**: 50 email signups before App Store launch

### 1.2 Seed Initial Schools

**Priority Targets:**
1. **Dan's daughter's preschool** - First real-world test case
2. **Friends/family with preschoolers** - Ask if their school would trial
3. **Local in-home daycares** - Easiest to onboard (small, flexible)

**Outreach Template:**
```
Subject: Free photo sharing app for [School Name]

Hi [Name],

I'm building Peekaboo, a secure photo-sharing app for preschools. It's free for parents and does one thing: let teachers share photos that only reach the right families.

I'm looking for 3 schools to try it before we launch publicly. Would you be open to a 10-minute demo?

[Signature]
```

### 1.3 App Store Optimization (Pre-Launch)

**Complete before submission:**
- [ ] Finalize app store screenshots (5 per platform)
- [ ] Record 30-second app preview video
- [ ] Prepare app icon in all required sizes
- [ ] Write localized descriptions (English first)
- [ ] Select final keyword set (see GROWTH_RESEARCH.md)

## Phase 2: Launch Week (Week 3)

### 2.1 App Store Launch Timing

**Recommended Launch Day**: Tuesday or Wednesday
- Avoid Monday (weekend backlog for reviewers)
- Avoid Friday (low download activity over weekends)
- Tuesday/Wednesday maximize visibility in weekly charts

### 2.2 Launch Announcement Channels

| Channel | Content | Goal |
|---------|---------|------|
| Email list | "We're live! Download now" | Drive initial downloads |
| Personal social | LinkedIn/Facebook post | Tap personal network |
| Teacher Facebook groups | Soft intro, not promotional | Awareness, beta testers |
| Preschool director forums | Value-first posts about photo privacy | Thought leadership |
| Product Hunt | Simple listing | Tech-savvy parents |

### 2.3 Launch Week Push

**Day 1 (Launch)**:
- Email waiting list
- Personal social posts (Dan + any team)
- Text pilot schools to download & review

**Day 2-3**:
- Follow up with pilot schools for App Store reviews
- Share any early testimonials on social

**Day 4-7**:
- Post in 2-3 relevant Facebook groups
- Submit to Product Hunt (or schedule for following week)

## Phase 3: Post-Launch Growth (Weeks 4-12)

### 3.1 Review Velocity Strategy

**Goal**: 50 five-star reviews in first 90 days

**Tactics:**
1. **In-app review prompt** - Trigger after 3rd photo view (positive experience moment)
2. **Email follow-up** - Week 2: "How's Peekaboo working? Leave us a review!"
3. **Teacher incentive** - "5 reviews from your parents = free Peekaboo swag"

**Review Request Email (for teachers to forward):**
```
Subject: Quick favor — help Peekaboo grow

Hi parents,

We've been using Peekaboo to share classroom photos, and we love it! If you're enjoying it too, would you take 30 seconds to leave a review on the App Store?

[Link to App Store]

It really helps other preschools find us. Thank you!

— [Teacher Name]
```

### 3.2 Content Marketing

**Blog/Social Content Ideas:**

| Topic | Angle | Target Audience |
|-------|-------|-----------------|
| "5 ways preschools leak kids' photos without knowing" | Fear/education | Directors, privacy-conscious parents |
| "Why we strip EXIF data from every photo" | Technical transparency | Privacy advocates, tech parents |
| "What I learned sharing 1,000 preschool photos" | Teacher voice | Teachers considering adoption |
| "The hidden cost of per-child pricing" | Competitive | Directors comparing options |

**Distribution:**
- Company blog (peekaboo.photos/blog when ready)
- Medium publication
- LinkedIn articles
- Teacher Facebook groups (with permission)

### 3.3 Referral Program (Simple v1)

**Concept**: School-to-school referral

**Mechanic:**
- Current school refers a new school
- Both schools get 1 month free on their next tier
- Tracked via referral code (e.g., "SUNSHINE-PRESCHOOL")

**Implementation**: Can be manual tracking in v1 (spreadsheet), automated later

### 3.4 Partnership Opportunities

| Partner Type | Value Exchange | Priority |
|--------------|----------------|----------|
| Childcare associations | We present at conferences, they promote to members | High |
| Teacher supply companies | Cross-promote to their mailing lists | Medium |
| Photography mini-session businesses | "Easy handoff to parents" angle | Medium |
| Child development bloggers | Free access in exchange for honest review | Low |

## Phase 4: Scaling (Months 4-12)

### 4.1 Paid Acquisition (When Ready)

**Start paid ads only after:**
- [ ] 100+ organic downloads
- [ ] 4.5+ App Store rating with 50+ reviews
- [ ] Proven retention (50%+ Day 7 retention)

**Initial Budget**: $500/month test

**Channels to Test:**
1. **Facebook/Instagram** - Target parents with children aged 2-5
2. **Google App Campaigns** - Target "daycare app" searches
3. **Pinterest** - Parenting/childcare boards

**Avoid Initially:**
- TikTok (audience skews too young for preschool parents)
- Twitter/X (low ROI for this demographic)

### 4.2 PR & Media

**Story Angles:**
1. "Dad builds privacy-first app for daughter's preschool"
2. "How one photo sharing app is fighting back against child data brokers"
3. "The Great Unbundling: Why preschools don't need all-in-one software"

**Target Outlets:**
- Local news (origin story)
- TechCrunch (if there's a funding angle)
- Parenting publications (Parents, Scary Mommy, Today's Parent)
- EdTech publications (EdSurge, The Journal)

### 4.3 Trade Shows & Conferences

**High-Value Events:**
- NAEYC Annual Conference (National Association for the Education of Young Children)
- State-level childcare provider conferences
- Early childhood education expos

**Booth Strategy (Low Budget):**
- Small banner + iPad demo
- QR code linking to free trial
- "Spin to win" for free merchandise
- Collect emails for follow-up

## Appendix: Messaging Framework

### Positioning Statement

> Peekaboo is the secure photo-sharing app for preschools that puts privacy first. Unlike all-in-one platforms that bundle features you don't need, Peekaboo does one thing perfectly: connecting parents to their child's day through photos they can trust are protected.

### Key Messages

| Audience | Primary Message |
|----------|-----------------|
| Parents | "See your child's day. Know their photos are safe." |
| Teachers | "Share photos in seconds. We handle the privacy." |
| Directors | "Flat-rate pricing. No per-child fees. Happy parents." |

### Proof Points

1. **Security**: "We strip location data from every photo automatically"
2. **Privacy**: "Parents only see photos of their own child—enforced at the database level"
3. **Simplicity**: "No passwords. No complicated setup. Just tap to sign in."
4. **Value**: "Free for parents. Always."

### Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We already use Brightwheel" | "Brightwheel is great for billing and attendance. Peekaboo does one thing—photos—better. Many schools use both." |
| "Is it secure?" | "More secure than email or text. Photos are encrypted, links expire, and we never store location data." |
| "We don't need another app" | "Parents don't need to download anything. They view photos in their browser. Teachers just need one app—Peekaboo." |
| "How do I know you won't sell data?" | "We're privacy-first by design. Our business model is subscriptions, not data. We have no advertising." |
