## Privacy-First Photo Sharing App for Preschools Launches to Help Parents See Their Child's Day

*Peekaboo strips location data, enforces parent-child access at the database level, and eliminates passwords—all free for parents*

---

**[LOCATION], March 2026** — Peekaboo, a new secure photo-sharing app designed specifically for preschools and daycares, launches today on iOS, Android, and web. The app addresses growing parental concerns about child photo privacy while giving teachers a simple way to share daily classroom moments.

Unlike all-in-one childcare management platforms where photo sharing is an afterthought, Peekaboo focuses exclusively on secure photo sharing—doing one thing well rather than many things adequately.

### The Privacy Problem

"I was getting classroom photos by email and noticed they included GPS coordinates and other families' children," said Dan Sickles, Peekaboo's founder. "As a parent, I wanted to see my daughter's day. As a software engineer, I knew there had to be a better way."

Research shows that standard photo files contain embedded metadata (EXIF data) including precise location coordinates, timestamps, and device information. When shared via email or generic messaging apps, this data travels with the image—a privacy risk most parents don't know exists.

### How Peekaboo Is Different

**For Parents:**
- See only photos of your own child (enforced at the database level, not just the app)
- No passwords—sign in with a secure magic link sent to your email
- All location and device metadata automatically stripped before storage
- Photo links expire after 15 minutes, preventing unauthorized sharing
- Always free for families

**For Teachers:**
- Upload photos in seconds from any device
- Tag children with one tap so the right families see each photo
- No complicated setup or training required
- Flat-rate pricing with no per-child fees

### Security by Design

Peekaboo's security approach goes beyond typical app protections:

- **Row-Level Security**: Access controls are enforced at the database level, meaning parents physically cannot request photos of children they're not linked to—even if they tried to manipulate the app
- **Automatic EXIF Stripping**: GPS coordinates, camera information, and timestamps are removed from every photo before it's stored
- **Signed Expiring URLs**: Photo links become invalid after 15 minutes, preventing screenshots from being shared as working links
- **Magic Link Authentication**: No passwords to steal, guess, or reuse across breached services

### Pricing

Peekaboo is free for parents—always. Schools can start free with up to 25 children, with paid tiers starting at $19/month for growing programs. Unlike competitors that charge per child, Peekaboo uses flat-rate pricing that doesn't penalize schools for growing enrollment.

Schools interested in piloting Peekaboo can sign up for free at peekaboo.photos.
