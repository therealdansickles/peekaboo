# Peekaboo Growth Research

**Last Updated**: March 13, 2026
**Prepared by**: Growth Agent (Paperclip)

---

## Executive Summary

Peekaboo enters a mature but fragmented preschool photo-sharing market dominated by all-in-one childcare management platforms. The top competitors (Brightwheel, Playground, Lillio, Daily Connect, Tadpoles) bundle photo sharing with billing, attendance, and compliance features. Peekaboo's **focused value proposition**—secure, privacy-first photo sharing with RLS-enforced access—represents a differentiated entry point, particularly for schools that already have administrative software but want a better parent photo experience.

---

### 1. Brightwheel

**App Store Rating**: 4.9/5 (123,820 ratings)

**Overview**: Market leader. All-in-one platform combining billing, attendance, communication, lesson planning, and reporting.

**Strengths**:
- Massive user base and brand recognition
- Real-time photo, video, and milestone sharing
- Robust billing engine with 90% on-time payment rate
- Free for parents to use
- Saves centers ~20 hours/month on admin
- Spanish language support

**Weaknesses**:
- Limited to 10 photos per upload batch
- Parents get separate notifications for each photo (annoying)
- Difficult message threading for teachers
- "Clearly designed without teacher input"
- Parents cannot use photos from their library
- No video sharing capability for parents
- Per-child pricing scales with enrollment

**Pricing**: Custom pricing, roughly $2/child/month. Credit card processing: 3.5%. Scales from ~$36/mo (10 kids) to $1,800/mo for large centers.

**Market Position**: Dominant incumbent. Hard to displace, but criticized for UX issues.

---

### 2. Playground

**App Store Rating**: 5.0/5 (3,800 ratings)

**Overview**: Fast-growing challenger to Brightwheel. All-in-one platform with strong customer service reputation.

**Strengths**:
- Perfect 5.0 App Store rating
- #1 rated for customer support
- Predictive enrollment feature (unique)
- No fee for billing software
- Saves 25+ hours/month on enrollment/billing
- Multi-site support
- CACFP-compliant food program integration
- Strong data security reputation

**Weaknesses**:
- Smaller user base than Brightwheel
- Complex billing setup initially
- Missing some key reports (document expiration tracking)
- No demo site before purchase
- Limited payment processing customization

**Pricing**: Free billing software. Custom pricing for full platform. Often positioned as Brightwheel alternative.

**Market Position**: Fastest-growing alternative. Strong word-of-mouth from customer service.

---

### 3. Lillio (formerly HiMama)

**App Store Rating**: 4.9/5 (37,162 ratings)

**Overview**: Canadian-based platform emphasizing curriculum alignment and developmental tracking.

**Strengths**:
- 92% user satisfaction rating
- NAEYC-aligned observation tools
- Research-based curriculum content
- Strong parent portal (98% importance rating)
- Detailed daily reports
- Multiple children view for parents
- Does not sell user data; AWS-hosted

**Weaknesses**:
- Limited photo/video downloads (especially iOS)
- App sometimes slow to load
- No sleep timer (one-button start/stop)
- Steeper learning curve
- Occasional notification lag
- Video files slow to load

**Pricing**: Custom pricing (not publicly listed). Free trial available. Free for parents.

**Market Position**: Strong in curriculum-focused centers. Canadian origins give privacy credibility.

---

### 4. Daily Connect

**App Store Rating**: 4.8/5 (13,106 ratings)

**Overview**: Used by 90,000+ childcare professionals. Focus on real-time updates and daily reporting.

**Strengths**:
- Large global user base (90K+ professionals)
- QR code contactless sign-in
- 50+ built-in learning frameworks
- Digital portfolios for children
- Apple Watch support
- Affordable and customizable

**Weaknesses**:
- "Not very Droid friendly"
- Text window hides under keyboard on Android
- App kicks students out of classrooms unexpectedly
- "Looks cheap and tacky" (per some reviews)
- Works "50% of the time" (reliability issues)
- Shift from one-time purchase to subscription caused backlash

**Pricing**: Free + in-app purchases ($4.99-$79.99 subscription tiers).

**Market Position**: Budget-friendly option. Reliability concerns limit growth.

### 5. Tadpoles (by Teaching Strategies)

**App Store Rating**: ~4.5/5 (2,600+ programs)

**Overview**: Pioneer in daycare communication. Mobile-first daily reporting and photo sharing.

**Strengths**:
- 300,000+ families using the platform
- User-friendly for both teachers and parents
- Real-time updates and secure photo/video sharing
- Good customer support (24/7 live support)
- Free trial available

**Weaknesses**:
- Outdated app design ("looks like iPhone 1 era")
- Requires constant refreshing to see updates
- "Unavailable" outages frustrate parents
- Very slow customer service response times
- Lacks billing automation, CRM, multilingual support
- No multi-site scalability
- Not keeping pace with modern all-in-one platforms

**Pricing**: Custom pricing (contact sales). Free trial available.

**Market Position**: Legacy player losing ground. Strong in small-to-mid programs but not evolving.

## 3. Recommended Pricing Strategy

### Market Context

The childcare software market uses three primary models:

1. **Per-child pricing** (Brightwheel): ~$2/child/month. Scales with enrollment.
2. **Flat monthly fee** (Procare): ~$25/month for small centers.
3. **Freemium** (MyKidReports): Free up to 10 children, then $0.99/child.

### Peekaboo Pricing Recommendation: **Freemium with School-Level Tiers**

Given Peekaboo's focused scope (photo sharing only, not full management) and new market entry, a freemium model maximizes adoption while establishing value.

---

### Tier Structure

| Tier | Price | Includes | Target |
|------|-------|----------|--------|
| **Free** | $0/month | Up to 25 children, unlimited photos, 1 classroom, basic support | In-home daycares, trial users |
| **Starter** | $19/month | Up to 75 children, unlimited photos, 3 classrooms, email support | Small preschools |
| **Growth** | $49/month | Up to 200 children, unlimited photos, unlimited classrooms, priority support, analytics | Medium centers |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | Unlimited everything, multi-site, dedicated success manager, SLA | Large center networks |

---

### Key Pricing Principles

1. **Parents always free**: Parents should never pay. This is industry standard and removes adoption friction.

2. **No per-child fees**: Differentiate from Brightwheel. "Flat-rate pricing that doesn't punish growth."

3. **Generous free tier**: 25 children covers most in-home providers and creates word-of-mouth ambassadors.

4. **Annual discount**: Offer 2 months free (17% discount) for annual commitment.

5. **Non-profit discount**: 20% off for verified 501(c)(3) organizations.

---

### Why Freemium Over Per-Child

| Per-Child Model | Freemium Model |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Predictable per-user revenue | Higher adoption, viral growth |
| Scales with center size | Predictable costs for schools |
| Centers resent "growth tax" | Schools love unlimited model |
| Harder to convert trials | Easy upsell path (classroom limits) |

Peekaboo's photo-only focus means lower infrastructure costs per user than full management platforms, making freemium viable.

## 4. Key Takeaways & Recommendations

### For App Store Launch

1. **Lead with privacy**: EXIF stripping, signed URLs, RLS-enforced access are genuine differentiators. Use "secure," "private," and "protected" in all copy.

2. **Target Brightwheel frustrations**: Position as "the photo app Brightwheel should have been." Focus on teacher UX and unlimited uploads.

3. **Mobile-first messaging**: 95% mobile users means every screenshot, every feature, must demonstrate phone-native experience.

4. **Parent testimonials matter**: Parents choose based on what makes them feel connected to their child's day.

### For Growth Strategy

1. **Free tier is acquisition**: The free tier pays for itself in word-of-mouth and App Store reviews.

2. **Teacher champions drive adoption**: If teachers love it, they recommend it to their next school.

3. **Build for privacy-conscious parents**: Post-Cambridge Analytica, parents care about data. "We never sell your photos" is a selling point.

4. **Don't compete on features—compete on focus**: Brightwheel does everything. Peekaboo does photos *perfectly*.
